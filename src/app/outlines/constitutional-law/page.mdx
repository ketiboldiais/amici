# Constitutional Law
## State Action Doctrine
- For a court to hear a constitutional violation claim, there must be a federal, state, or local government action; the Constitution does not control the behavior of private citizens or corporations.[^state_action_origin]
- __Private Actors.__ A private actor’s actions may be considered State action if:
	1. the private actions carry out functions traditionally exclusively reserved to the State.[^jackson]
		- _Example_. The private management of a company town.[^marsh]
		- _Example_. The private management of elections for public office.[^terry_v_adams]
	2. the state is heavily involved in carrying out the actions.[^shelley_v_kraemer]
		- _Example_. The actions occur on government property, with the government’s permission.[^burton]
		- _Example_. The state actively encourages the action.[^reitman]
		- _Example_. The state enforces private decisions.[^shelley_v_kraemer_2]

[^state_action_origin]: _See_ _Franz v. United States_, 707 F.2d 582, 591 n.33 (D.C. Cir.), _modified_, 712 F.2d 1428 (D.C. Cir. 1983); ~L. Tribe~, ~American Constitutional Law~ 1148 n.2 (1978). _See also_ _Virginia v. Rives_, 100 U.S. 313, 318 (1879); _United States v. Cruikshank_, 92 U.S. 542, 554-55 (1875); _Civil Rights Cases_, 109 U.S. 3 (1883); ~J. Nowak, R. Rotunda, & J. Young~, ~Constitutional Law~, 501-02 (2d. ed. 1983) (“If the Court finds that the alleged wrongdoer is not involved with state action, it will afford no remedy to the aggrieved party.”).
[^jackson]: _Jackson v. Metropolitan Edison Co._, 419 U.S. 345, 352 (1974).
[^marsh]: _March v. Alabama_, 326 U.S. 461, _reh'g denied_, 322 U.S. 769 (1944).
[^terry_v_adams]: _Terry v. Adams_, 345 U.S. 461, _reh’g denied_, 345 U.S. 1003 (1953). 
[^shelley_v_kraemer]: _Shelly v. Kraemer_, 334 U.S. 1 (1948).
[^burton]: _Burton v. Wilmington Parking Authority_, 365 U.S. 715 (1961).
[^reitman]: _Reitman v. Mulkey_, 387 U.S. 369 (1967).
[^shelley_v_kraemer_2]: _Shelly v. Kraemer_, 334 U.S. 1 (1948).
